A gymnasium, also known as a "gym", is an indoor location for athletics. The word is derived from the ancient Greek term "gymnasium". They are commonlyfound in athletic and fitness centers, and as activity and learning spaces in educational institutions. "Gym" is also slang for "fitness centre", which is often an area for indoor recreation.
A "gym" may include or describe adjacent open air areas as well. In Western countries, "gyms" (or pl: gymnasia") often describe places with indoor or outdoor courts for basketball, hockey, tennis, boxing or wrestling, and with equipment and machines used for physical development training, or to do exercises. In many European countries, Gymnasium (and variations of the word) also can describe a secondary school that prepares students for higher education at a university, with or without the presence of athletic courts, fields, or equipment.









How do I start fitness at the gym ?

SEATED LEG PRESS

Seated leg press is done by Place your feet on the platform about shoulder-width apart, and grasp the handles with your hands. Slowly push the platform away with your legs. Keep your feet firmly planted on the platform and stop just short of full knee extension. Then slowly return to the starting position. (10 reps * 3 sets).

SEATED SHOULDER PRESS

Seated shoulder press is done by Sit on the bench holding two dumbbells at shoulder height with an overhand grip. Press the weights up above your head until your arms are fully extended. Return slowly to the start position.(10 reps*3 sets)

BODYWEIGHT LUNGES

Bodyweight lunge is Step forward with one leg and allow both knees to bend simultaneously. Descend until the back knee touches the floor. Drive through the front foot and extend the knee as you stand up fully and return to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.(10 reps * 3sets).

WIDE PUSHUPS

Perform wide push-ups by placing your palms a few inches wider than shoulder-width apart on the floor and holding yourself in a high plank position. While keeping your back and legs in a straight line, lower your body toward the floor, then push back up to the starting position.(10 reps * 3sets).

PLANK

The plank (also called a front hold, hover, or abdominal bridge) is an isometric core strength exercise that involves maintaining a position similar to a push-up for the maximum possible time .(30 secs * 3 sets).
